Projects

Software Engineering: Course Registration Platform -- 2018

  • Developed a web application on a team of 6, to assist instructors at WPI in registering for courses to teach.
  • Utilized resource gathering techniques to identify the functionality needed by the app.
  • The app was developed using the Spring framework. Database was created in PostgreSQL (pgAdmin IDE). Back-end created in Java (Eclipse IDE). Front-end created in Google Web Toolkit.
  • I helped create each layer of the application (DB, Back-end, Front-end) while also being a project manager for the team.

Interactive Qualifying Project (IQP) -- 2017-2018

  • A 14-week project where my team of 4 collaborated directly with a sponsor company, in China, to solve real-world problems they faced. Our team provided the sponsor company with several major deliverables, which, were immediately integrated into company operations and products.
  • Created a 200+ page research document for the company, outlining the current state of the industry they work in and making recommendations to improve performance of the company.
  • My role on the team was that of a project manager.

Unified Robotics III: 3-DOF Robot Arm -- 2017

  • Developed a real-time processor program with a team of 4, to control a 3-DOF robot arm to sort specific objects by their weight and color with the use of visual tracking and force sensors. 
  • Focused on embedded computing and dynamic [feedback] response as it relates to motion planning.
  • Utilized forward and inverse kinematics, forward and inverse velocity kinematics and actuator design to develop trajectory planning functions for a 3-DOF arm.

Unified Robotics II: Flame Extinguishing Robot -- 2016

  • Designed an autonomous robot with a team of 4, that navigates a structured environment, finds the simulated fire (a candle), reports the location of the simulated fire, and finally, extinguishes the flame before returning back to its original starting location.
  • To navigate the field, the robot relies on two infrared sensors, in addition to an IMU (Inertial Measurement Unit) to determine the heading of the robot in relation to the robot’s origin.

Unified Robotics I: Nuclear Reactor Robot -- 2016

  • Designed an autonomous system on a team of 4, that can pick up spent nuclear rods from a reactor, store them in a safe area, and refuel the reactor with a fresh rod.
  • To navigate the field, the robot relies on a Bluetooth connection. The field relays information about the field itself, such as the location of new rods and the availability of storage for used rods.
